Submarine Commander

This book is an autobiography by LCDR Paul Schratz about his time in the US Navy captaining submarines. During WW2, being a submariner in the pacific was the most dangerous job anyone could have. The book is really insightful.

He goes through how he decides what to put in the sub, and then how he commands it, how he hunts ships, avoids anti-submarine vessels, and numerous close calls. He also brags about how he has done the longest submerged travel in a diesel-electric submarine, with the impressive time of 21 days using the snorkelers, which he did in the USS Pickerel, after WW2. He also had one of the most successful volleyball teams for the Pacific Submariners!

I wouldn't call it the best book I've ever read (hence why this is so short and not the best), but it was pretty good.
