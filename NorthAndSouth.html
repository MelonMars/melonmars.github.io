North And South

This book was... interesting. I thought it was kind of weird, to say the least. It basically seemed to me like someone from Southern England trying to make Northern England seem all stuck up rude.

At the same time, it did give a good glance into how England actually was around the mid-1800s, even though it did seem a little dishonest, it did seem pretty honest to. And it matches up with other stuff I've read from around that time.

The book, in short, is about a girl from rural Southern England, moving to an industrial town in urban Northern England, and not liking it there, but also falling in love with a factory owner, who she doesn't like. She also makes friends with a sickly young girl dying (apparently due to pollution), while her mom is also dying, and her brother, who was a lieutenant on a boat that had a mutiny, which he led, comes back, under the threat of execution, to see them.

I think the book's outstanding for a more drama sort of book, but I don't really like drama, so I wasn't all that thrilled by it.
